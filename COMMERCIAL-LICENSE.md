## FAQ

**Is JClaw open source?**
No. JClaw is *source-available*. The PolyForm Noncommercial License restricts
commercial use, which means it does not meet the Open Source Definition.
Versions up to and including v0.15.4 were released under the MIT License and
remain available under that license; versions from v0.16.0 onward are
dual-licensed as described here.

**Can I evaluate JClaw before buying?**
Yes. Evaluation and testing without any anticipated commercial application is
a permitted noncommercial purpose under the PolyForm Noncommercial License.

**Can I use JClaw in my commercial product or internal business operations
without a license if I don't distribute it?**
No. Internal business use is commercial use.

**I'm a non-profit / university / government body. Do I need a license?**
No. Use by charitable organizations, educational institutions, public research
organizations, public safety or health organizations, environmental protection
organizations, and government institutions is a permitted purpose under the
noncommercial license, regardless of funding source.
